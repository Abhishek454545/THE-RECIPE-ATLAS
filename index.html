GULAB JAUMUN

INGREDIENTS:

  • Milk powder
  • All-purpose flour or wheat flour
  • Baking soda or baking powder
  • Ghee
  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Cardamom powder
  • Rose water (optional)
  • Oil or ghee

STEPS TO MAKE:

Step 1: Make the Sugar Syrup
1) In a pan, add sugar and water.
2) Heat until the sugar dissolves completely.

Kaju katli

INGREDIENTS:

  • Cashew nuts
  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Milk powder
  • Cardamom powder
  • Silver leaf
  • Pistachios
  • Rose water (optional)

STEPS TO MAKE:

Step 1: Prepare the Cashew Powder
1) Take cashews (kaju) and ensure they are at room temperature ‚Äî not refrigerated.
2) Grind them in a mixer to a fine powder.
3) Pulse in short intervals ‚Äî don‚Äôt over-grind, or the cashews will release oil.
4) Sieve the powder to remove any coarse pieces.

Step 2: Make the Sugar Syrup
1) In a non-stick pan, add sugar and water (use just enough water to dissolve the sugar).
2) Heat on medium flame until the sugar dissolves completely.
3) Continue boiling until you get a one-string consistency ‚Äî when a small drop between your fingers stretches into a single thread.
4) Lower the flame immediately once it reaches this stage.

Step 3: Add Cashew Powder
1) Add the cashew powder to the syrup gradually while stirring continuously.
2) Mix well to avoid lumps and cook on low flame.
3) Keep stirring until the mixture thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan.
4) To check ‚Äî take a small portion, roll it between your fingers; if it forms a soft ball, it‚Äôs ready.

Step 4: Knead the Dough
1) Turn off the flame and transfer the mixture onto a greased plate or butter paper.
2) Let it cool slightly (warm, not hot).
3) Add 1 tsp ghee (optional) and knead gently until smooth.

Step 5: Roll and Cut the Katlis
1) Place the dough between two sheets of butter paper.
2) Roll it evenly into a thin layer (about 3‚Äì5 mm thick).
3) Let it cool for a few minutes, then cut into diamond-shaped pieces using a sharp knife.
4) Optionally, apply varak (edible silver leaf) on top for a traditional look.4) If it‚Äôs sticky, let it rest for a few minutes before rolling.

MOHANTHAL

INGREDIENTS:

  • Coarse gram flour (besan)
  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Milk
  • Cardamom powder
  • Silver leaf
  • Pistachios
  • Saffron

STEPS TO MAKE:

Step 1: Prepare the Gram Flour Mixture
In a large bowl, take besan (gram flour).
Add 2‚Äì3 tbsp ghee and 1‚Äì2 tbsp milk to it.
Mix well with your hands until the flour looks coarse and crumbly (this process is called moyan).
Sieve the mixture through a coarse sieve to remove any lumps.

Step 2: Roast the Flour
Heat ¬Ω cup ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan on low flame.
Add the prepared besan mixture.
Roast slowly on low flame, stirring continuously.
Roast until the mixture turns golden brown and gives off a nutty aroma (this takes around 10‚Äì12 minutes).
Turn off the heat and let it cool slightly.

Step 3: Make the Sugar Syrup
In another pan, add sugar and water (just enough to dissolve the sugar).
Heat until the sugar melts completely.
Boil till you get a one-string consistency ‚Äî a single thread forms when you stretch the syrup between your fingers.
Add a few strands of saffron and cardamom powder for flavor.
Turn off the flame.

Step 4: Combine Mixture and Syrup
Pour the sugar syrup gradually into the roasted besan mixture.
Stir continuously to avoid lumps.
Mix until everything blends well and starts to thicken.
Add a few tablespoons of ghee while mixing ‚Äî this gives the mohanthal a rich texture.

Step 5: Set the Mohanthal
Grease a square or rectangular tray with ghee.
Pour the hot mixture into the tray and spread it evenly.
Sprinkle chopped almonds, pistachios, or silver varak on top.
Press gently with a spatula and let it cool completely.
Once set, cut into squares or diamonds.

Serve your Mohanthal at room temperature or slightly warm.
It stays fresh for several days in an airtight container and is perfect for festive occasions!
